Publishing directly to the LOR has many benefits, the biggest being able to upload files from your computer. If you plan on using the object in several courses, it may be advantageous to publish it in this way and retrieve it later. At the top of this page you will find a narrated video demonstrating these steps. Below you will find the steps written with accompanying screenshots.

Step 1

From your Dashboard, click on a course.

Step 2

From the Navbar choose Tools

Step 3

Click “LOR” from the Tools Menu

Step 4

Below the Navbar you will see two options: “Search” and “Publish”

Step 5

Click “Publish”

Step 6

When you publish using this method there are only two sources from which you can publish from.

The first is uploading a file from your computer, which could be a document, photo, video, etc. (A)

The second is you can specify an internet address or URL to a specific object, which can be useful if your file is hosted elsewhere. (B)

You can also publish a website address itself, which will turn into a link to that website.

Step 7

Choose the desired method: A file (A) or an Internet address (B)

Step 8

Choose to publish to a specific repository (A) or overwrite an existing object (B).

Click the "Next" button (C)

Note: Overwriting an object is reviewed later in this module. Click here to access it. We will continue these steps by publishing the object as new.

Step 9

Fill out the required metadata fields including: title, description, and keywords that describe and categorize your learning object.

Click here to access the module on metadata.

Step 10

If you are uploading an image, choose your desired thumbnail (A) for the object at the bottom of the page.

Then, click the "Next" button (B)

Step 11

Your object has now been published to the LOR.

Click the "Done" button finish
